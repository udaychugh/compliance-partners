Complaints Process

We value feedback and view complaints as an opportunity to improve. This page outlines how we handle issues and how you can submit a complaint.

Last Updated: July 13, 2026

1. Our Commitment

At NDIS Compliance Partners, we aim to provide high-quality compliance, governance, and business registration support services to NDIS providers across Australia. If we fail to meet your expectations, we encourage you to let us know. We treat all complaints seriously, confidentially, and with fairness.

2. How to Make a Complaint

If you wish to raise a complaint or provide feedback, you can contact us through any of the following methods:

  • Email: send your feedback directly to contact@lgpartners.com.au.
  • Phone: call us on 0498475578 during business hours.

3. Our Resolution Process

We follow a structured process to ensure all complaints are addressed efficiently:

Step 1: Acknowledgment

We will acknowledge your complaint within 2 business days of receipt.

Step 2: Investigation & Review

We will investigate the circumstances surrounding your complaint. This may involve speaking with the staff involved, reviewing documentation, and identifying the root cause of the issue.

Step 3: Response & Action

We will propose a resolution or provide our findings to you within 10 business days. We will discuss proposed actions, such as correcting errors, adjusting services, or updating internal procedures.

Step 4: Continuous Improvement

We register all complaints in our internal quality register to ensure we implement changes that prevent recurring issues.

4. External Escalation

If you are not satisfied with our response, or if you feel the complaint cannot be resolved directly with us, you have options for external review:

  • For issues related to consumer protection, billing, or services, you can contact your local State Consumer Affairs agency.
  • If you are a participant or a provider and have concerns regarding NDIS compliance advice that impacts participant safety, quality, or compliance with NDIS Practice Standards, you can contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission:
    Phone: 1800 035 544
    Website: ndiscommission.gov.au
