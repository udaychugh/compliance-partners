Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us. This policy details how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth).

Last Updated: July 13, 2026

1. Introduction

NDIS Compliance Partners ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, hold, use, and disclose your personal information. It applies to all clients, website visitors, and individuals who interact with our services.

By using our website, services, or otherwise providing us with your personal information, you consent to the collection, use, and disclosure of your personal information as described in this policy.

2. Personal Information We Collect

We may collect personal information that is reasonably necessary to provide our services and manage our business relationships. This may include:

  • Contact details (e.g. name, organization, position, email address, telephone number, and physical address).
  • Business information (e.g. details regarding your NDIS provider status, business structure, registration type, current policies, and compliance procedures).
  • Financial information (e.g. billing information, credit card or bank details for invoicing and transaction purposes).
  • Technical data (e.g. IP address, browser type, device information, and website usage statistics via cookies and analytics tools).

3. How We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information in several ways, including:

  • Directly from you when you submit a contact or inquiry form on our website.
  • During consultations, meetings, phone calls, or email correspondence.
  • When you engage us to perform advisory, audit preparation, or business registration services.
  • From publicly available sources, such as the NDIS Provider Register or corporate registers.

4. Purpose of Collection and Use

We collect and use personal information to:

  • Provide our consulting, governance, risk, compliance, finance, and business advisory services.
  • Respond to your inquiries, schedule consultations, and maintain contact.
  • Manage administrative matters, including invoicing, billing, and processing payments.
  • Improve our website, services, and client experience.
  • Comply with our legal and regulatory obligations.

5. Disclosure of Personal Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may disclose your personal information to:

  • Our employees, contractors, and professional advisors who need the information to perform services.
  • Third-party service providers (e.g. hosting providers, payment processors, and IT systems) that help us operate our business.
  • Government bodies or regulatory authorities (such as the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission, ASIC, or the ATO) where required or authorized by law.

6. Data Security

We take reasonable physical, electronic, and managerial steps to safeguard your personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorized access, modification, or disclosure. However, no data transmission over the internet can be guaranteed as completely secure.

7. Access and Correction

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to request corrections if you believe it is inaccurate, out of date, or incomplete. To make a request, please contact us using the details below.
